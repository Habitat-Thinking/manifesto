# Contributing to the Habitat Thinking Manifesto

Thank you for caring enough about the place we build in to want to improve how it is described. This is a manifesto — a piece of writing, not a piece of software — so contributing here is less about code and more about language, clarity, and the ideas the words carry. The bar is simple: does your change help the manifesto say what it means more truthfully, or more clearly, to the people and agents who will read it?

## What contributions are welcome

- **Clarity and wording.** Tightening a sentence, fixing an awkward phrase, correcting a typo, or making a principle land more cleanly without changing its meaning.
- **Translations.** Faithful translations into other languages, kept as separate files so the canonical English remains intact.
- **Questions and challenges.** If a value or principle reads as wrong, contradictory, or unhelpful, open an issue. Disagreement argued in good faith is how the theory stays alive.
- **New or reworked principles.** Proposals that add, sharpen, or restructure the principles. These are the highest-stakes changes and get the most scrutiny — see below.

## How to contribute

1. **Open an issue first for anything beyond a typo.** A manifesto changes meaning easily, so it is worth agreeing on the intent before the wording. Describe what you think is unclear, missing, or wrong, and why.
2. **Fork and branch.** Make your change on a branch with a short, descriptive name.
3. **Keep edits focused.** One idea per pull request. A typo fix and a new principle do not belong in the same change.
4. **Explain the why.** In the pull request description, say what the change is trying to achieve, not just what it does. For wording changes, note whether you intend to change the meaning or only its expression.
5. **Open a pull request** against `main`.

## How changes are decided

The values and principles are deliberately stable. Cosmetic and clarity fixes are easy to accept. Changes to the *meaning* of a value or principle are a much bigger deal: they alter what the manifesto stands for, and they will be weighed slowly, in the open, and may be declined even when well argued. That is not a rejection of you — it is the manifesto doing its job of holding an orientation steady while tools and fashions turn over.

## Tone

Write the way the manifesto is written: plainly, for humans, in service of understanding. If a sentence is clever but harder to live by, prefer the clearer one.
