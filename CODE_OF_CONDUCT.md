# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

This project is about building places where every kind of intelligence in the room can think well together. We hold the conversation around it to the same standard. We pledge to make participation a respectful, harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Our standards

Behaviour that helps the place stay habitable:

- Engaging with ideas in good faith, and disagreeing without contempt.
- Giving and accepting honest, specific feedback gracefully.
- Assuming the other person is trying to make the work better, and arguing the point rather than the person.
- Taking responsibility, apologising to those affected by our mistakes, and learning from them.
- Focusing on what is best for the community and the work, not only for ourselves.

Behaviour that makes the place uninhabitable, and is not acceptable:

- Harassment of any kind, public or private.
- Insults, derogatory comments, or personal and political attacks.
- Trolling, deliberate provocation, or sustained disruption of discussion.
- Sexualised language or imagery, or unwelcome sexual attention.
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission.
- Any other conduct that could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces — issues, pull requests, discussions, and any other channels — and when an individual is officially representing the project in public spaces.

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behaviour may be reported to the project maintainers at **russ@russmiles.com**. All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly, and the privacy and security of the reporter will be respected.

Maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing these standards, and may take any action they deem appropriate — from a private warning to a temporary or permanent ban — in response to behaviour they consider inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.
